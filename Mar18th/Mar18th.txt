Customer Groups (Segmentation Result)

After applying K-Means clustering (K = 5) on the mall dataset based on Annual Income and Spending Score, customers are divided into the following groups:

1. High Income – High Spending (Premium Customers)

These customers have high income and high spending habits. They are the most valuable and loyal customers for the business.

Business Strategy:
Focus on premium services, loyalty programs, and exclusive offers. Target them with luxury products and personalized marketing.

2. High Income – Low Spending (Careful Customers)

These customers have high income but low spending behavior. They tend to be cautious or selective in their purchases.

Business Strategy:
Use discounts, special offers, and marketing campaigns to encourage them to spend more and become active buyers.

3. Low Income – High Spending (Impulsive Customers)

These customers have low income but high spending habits. They may be impulsive buyers.

Business Strategy:
Promote affordable deals and budget-friendly products. However, avoid excessive targeting as their long-term value may be limited.

4. Low Income – Low Spending (Budget Customers)

These customers have low income and low spending patterns. They contribute the least to overall revenue.

Business Strategy:
Provide basic and low-cost products. Limit high-cost marketing efforts for this group.

5. Average Income – Average Spending (Standard Customers)

These customers have moderate income and moderate spending behavior. They form a stable customer base.

Business Strategy:
Maintain customer satisfaction through regular offers, memberships, and consistent service.
